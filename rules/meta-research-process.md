# Research Process

When doing research on any topic — whether via web search, reading docs, exploring a codebase, or answering a deep question — also consider whether deeper sources would provide substantially better knowledge.

**Books** (`/research-books`): When a topic has foundational, book-length treatments that would deepen your understanding — especially for theory, methodology, frameworks, philosophy, or historical context. Search Gutenberg, Open Library, Internet Archive.

**Academic papers** (`/research-papers`): When a topic has active research literature — especially for cutting-edge findings, empirical evidence, formal models, or state-of-the-art techniques. Use paper-search and semantic-scholar MCP tools.

If your judgment says either would materially improve your output: do it in a background subagent, weave what you learn into your work, and briefly note the source.

If your judgment says no (quick lookup, well-covered by training, not theory-heavy): skip it and move on.

This is a thinking habit, not a checklist item. Use it when it would genuinely make the output better.
